1960s Design

The 1960s were a decade of upheaval. Around the world, former colonies became independent states, some peacefully, some through violence. In addition to territorial conflict, there was ideological conflict. The world was divided between capitalists, socialists and communists; democracies, military dictatorships and totalitarian, one-party states; between Western-bloc nations lead by the United States, and Eastern-bloc countries lead by the Soviet Union. Rivalry between the Superpowers impelled them to strive for global supremacy, creating in the process an arms race, and in turn, the ever-present threat of nuclear annihilation. The reaction to war and conflicting ideologies produced the non-aligned group of nations, and civilian agitation for peace in Western states. Within societies, people struggled for civil liberties, racial and gender equality, and sexual and reproductive rights. Anti-authoritarianism, non-conformity, freedom of expression, and artistic experimentation were the trends. It was the groovy, psychedelic decade. Through it all, Space continued to exert its influence, and fueled the race to put mankind on the Moon; culminating with the landing of America’s Apollo 11 Lunar Module on July 20th, 1969.
