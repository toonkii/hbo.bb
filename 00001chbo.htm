Mars has extreme temperature fluctuations from winter to summer compared to the Earth. It gets cold enough to freeze carbon dioxide out of the atmosphere during the winter, but this ice is unstable when warmer summer arrives and forces it to sublimate away.

Near the South Pole, however, it stays cold enough for some of this seasonal ice to stick around all year and even accumulate from year to year. This slab of ice is three meters (about 10 feet) thick and is penetrated by the flat-floored pits shown here. The quasi-circular pits in the center of the scene are about 60 meters (200 feet) across.
