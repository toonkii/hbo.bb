HELENE, a small, faint moon of Saturn, was discovered on March 1, 1980, by J. Lecacheux and P. Laques. It has a mean radius of 17.6km, and was originally designated S/1980 S6.

In keeping with John Herschel's suggestion that the moons of Saturn be associated with the mythical brothers and sisters of Kronos (Saturn's Greek equivalent), the International Astronomical Union named the moon after Saturn's granddaughter, the legendary cause of the Trojan War.
